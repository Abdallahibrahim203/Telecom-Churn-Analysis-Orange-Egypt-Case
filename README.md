
# Telecom Churn Analysis – Orange Egypt Case

Overview
This project analyzes telecom customer churn behavior using a real-world dataset,
presented as an Orange Egypt case study. The goal is to understand why customers
leave and identify the key factors driving churn.

Tools & Technologies
| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Python (Pandas) | Data Cleaning |
| Power BI | Dashboard & Visualization |
| Excel | Data Source |

Dataset
- Source: Kaggle – Telco Customer Churn Dataset
- Records: 7,043 customers
- Features: 21 columns including demographics, services, and billing info

Dashboard Pages

1. General Overview
High-level KPIs and customer distribution across internet service types, contract types, and demographics.

Key Metrics:
- Total Customers: 7,043
- Churned Customers: 1,869
- Churn Rate: 26.54%
- Average Monthly Charges: $32.37
- Average Total Charges: $64.76

2. Churn Dashboard
Deep dive into churned customers behavior and characteristics.

Highlights:
- Annual Revenue Lost: $2.86M
- Monthly Charges from Churned: $139.13K
- Tech Support Tickets: 2,173
- Admin Tickets: 885

3. Customer Risk Analysis
Identifies patterns and risk factors that predict churn.

Highlights:
- Annual Revenue at Risk: $16.06M
- Total Admin Tickets: 3,632
- Total Tech Tickets: 2,955

4. Key Insights
- Churn rate reached 26.54% — 1 in 4 customers left the company
- Customers on month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate at 42.71%
- Fiber Optic users churn more than any other internet type at 41.89%
- 55% of churned customers left within their first year
- Electronic check is the most used payment method among churned customers at 57.30%
- Customers without dependents or partners are more likely to churn
- Lack of services like Tech Support, Online Security, and Device Protection increases churn risk
- Gender has no significant impact on churn behavior
